## Overview

This template governs the disclosure of confidential information between SentinelCorp and a third party. Use this template for mutual NDAs. For one-way disclosure scenarios (e.g., the company disclosing to a vendor only), use the One-Way NDA template.

**Before using this template:**
1. Confirm the counterparty name and registered address from the company registry
2. Consult Legal if the agreement involves IP, source code, or customer data
3. All signed NDAs must be filed in the Legal contract management system (ContractPodAi) within 5 business days

## Article 1 — Purpose

The Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship regarding **[DESCRIBE PURPOSE, e.g., "a technology integration between the Parties' platforms"]** (the "Purpose"). In connection with the Purpose, each Party may disclose certain confidential information to the other Party.

---

## Article 2 — Confidential Information

### 2.1 Definition

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one Party ("**Disclosing Party**") to the other Party ("**Receiving Party**") in connection with the Purpose, whether disclosed in writing, orally, electronically, or by any other means, that:

(a) is designated as "confidential" or "proprietary" at the time of disclosure; or  
(b) a reasonable person would recognize as confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure.

### 2.2 Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) is or becomes publicly known other than through breach of this Agreement;  
(b) was in the Receiving Party's possession before receipt from the Disclosing Party, without restriction;  
(c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information;  
(d) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction; or  
(e) is required to be disclosed by applicable law, regulation, or court order, provided the Receiving Party provides prompt prior written notice to the Disclosing Party and cooperates with any efforts to seek a protective order.

---

## Article 3 — Obligations

### 3.1 Non-Disclosure

The Receiving Party shall:

(a) hold Confidential Information in strict confidence, using at minimum the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information (but no less than reasonable care);  
(b) use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and not for any other purpose;  
(c) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent of the Disclosing Party.

### 3.2 Authorized Disclosure

The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, advisors, and affiliates ("Representatives") who:

(a) have a need to know for the Purpose;  
(b) are bound by written confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than this Agreement.

The Receiving Party is responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives.

---

## Article 4 — Intellectual Property

Nothing in this Agreement grants either Party any license or ownership rights in the other Party's Confidential Information or intellectual property. All Confidential Information remains the exclusive property of the Disclosing Party.

---

## Article 5 — Term and Termination

### 5.1 Term

This Agreement commences on the date first written above and continues for **[2/3/5] years** unless earlier terminated by either Party upon **30 days** written notice.

### 5.2 Survival

Obligations under Article 3 survive termination of this Agreement for a period of **[3/5] years** with respect to information that constitutes a trade secret under applicable law, obligations survive indefinitely.

### 5.3 Return of Materials

Upon termination or written request by the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or certifiably destroy all Confidential Information and any copies thereof, and confirm destruction in writing.

---

## Article 6 — Remedies

The Parties acknowledge that breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be insufficient. Each Party is entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity.

---

## Article 7 — General Provisions

### 7.1 Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed under the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to conflict of law principles.

### 7.2 Dispute Resolution

Any dispute shall be resolved through binding arbitration under the rules of JAMS, with venue in San Francisco, California. Judgment may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

### 7.3 Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, or negotiations.

### 7.4 Amendment

This Agreement may only be amended by written instrument signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

### 7.5 Severability

If any provision is held invalid, the remaining provisions continue in full force and effect.
